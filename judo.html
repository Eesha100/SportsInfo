JUDO

1. Number of Players

Judo is an individual sport, typically played between two players, also known as judokas.

2. Strategy of the Game

The primary strategy in judo revolves around leveraging balance, momentum, and technique to throw, pin, or immobilize the opponent.

  • Offensive Techniques: Include throws (nage-waza) and joint locks.
  • Defensive Techniques: Focus on counters, escapes, and maintaining balance.
  • Control: Dominating the opponent through gripping techniques and tactical positioning.
  • Mental Strategy: Judokas anticipate and counter their opponent's moves while conserving energy.

3. Is Judo a team game?

While judo is fundamentally an individual sport, it can be played as a team competition in certain tournaments, where multiple players' results contribute to the team's overall score.

4. Rules of the Game

  • Matches take place on a tatami mat and usually last 4 minutes for men and women.
  • Points are awarded for throws, pins, or submission techniques.
    • Ippon: Instant victory for a perfect throw or submission.
    • Waza-ari: Half-point; two make an ippon.
  • Techniques targeting the joints (except the elbow) or striking are prohibited.
  • Fouls, such as stalling or illegal grips, can lead to penalties.

5. Important Facts about Judo

  • Origin: Judo was invented by Jigoro Kano in 1882 in Japan, as a form of modern martial art derived from jujitsu.
  • Olympic Sport: Judo has been an Olympic event since 1964, with women's judo added in 1992.
  • Special Tournaments:
    • The World Judo Championships is held annually.
    • Grand Slam Events and Grand Prix tournaments take place globally.
  • Philosophy: Judo means "the gentle way," focusing on mutual benefit and maximum efficiency.

6. Records and Statistics

  • Most World Titles (Men): Teddy Riner (France) with 11 titles.
  • Most Olympic Medals (Women): Ryoko Tani (Japan) with 5 medals (2 gold, 2 silver, 1 bronze).
  • First Olympic Champion: Tadahiro Nomura (Japan), who also won 3 consecutive Olympic golds.
  • Youngest Olympic Champion: Kosei Inoue (Japan) at 20 years old.

7. Countries Playing Judo

Judo is practiced worldwide. Key judo nations include:

  • Asia: Japan, South Korea, Mongolia, China.
  • Europe: France, Russia, Germany, Georgia.
  • Americas: Brazil, USA, Cuba.
  • Africa: Tunisia, Algeria, Egypt.
